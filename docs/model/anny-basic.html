1 · What it is

A risk-first entry/stop policy. It never places orders by itself — it proposes a side; the user confirms. It is intentionally selective: it would rather take no trade than a low-quality one.

4 · Campaign stops — why it makes few trades

The mission halts on the first of these (it does not force a trade count):

  • 🎯 Profit target reached → stop as success
  • ✋ 2 consecutive losses → stop (no revenge trading / no open-ended martingale)
  • ⏹ 15-trade hard cap → stop
A healthy ANNY Basic mission can legitimately be 2–4 trades. That is by design.

5 · Paper engine — how a /goal run is scored

A /goal run is a paper simulation on real Binance candles — no orders, no money — resolving each trade from real intrabar highs/lows.

  • Reward : Risk = 2 : 1, fixed (a 1:1 bracket is never traded).
  • Position size comes from the Leverage use % slider (decimal math; per-trade risk clamped to 20% of capital and to remaining drawdown).
  • capital_risk_pct = the cumulative plan-loss ceiling only — never leverage.
  • Fees (entry + exit) are deducted from every trade.
  • Edge / trade = realized PnL ÷ trades (fees included) — the RR-adjusted edge.

6 · "Launch ready" gate — what unlocks Next →

A paper result is launch ready when it shows a real edge, not a 1-trade fluke. Two ways to qualify — both require positive realized PnL and ≥ 2 trades:

PathCondition
Target hitVerdict = target_reached → launch ready (even with just 2 trades — hitting the goal is the proof)
Positive edge, no targetDidn't reach target, but proved positive edge over ≥ 5 trades
There is no "must make 5 trades" rule. The 5-trade floor applies only to runs that did not reach their target. A sparse ANNY Basic plan that hits its target in 2 winning trades is launch ready. (Launching to testnet still needs an active Binance key — the real-trading guard.)

Why win rate alone isn't the gate: at RR 2:1 the break-even win rate is 33%, so a high-RR plan can be profitable below 50% — the gate uses expectancy, not win rate.
